History

The roots of the modern rafting industry took hold in the post-WWII era, when a surplus of inflatable military bridge-building rafts allowed pioneers to explore rivers previously deemed "unrunnable." What began as a niche hobby for rugged explorers in the 1950s evolved into a professional industry during the 1970s, fueled by a growing cultural desire for authentic wilderness experiences. Today, the industry has transitioned from primitive rubber buckets to high-tech, self-bailing vessels, making the thrill of whitewater accessible to families and adrenaline-seekers alike while maintaining a rigorous standard of safety and conservation.
